About the Programme

What is the
Shadrach Fellowship?

The Shadrach Fellowship (TSF) is an intensive, one-year missional discipleship and leadership formation journey personally led by Dr. Yaw Perbi. It is named for the biblical Shadrach, a leader who refused to bow to compromise and stood firm in the furnace of his generation.

The Fellowship is designed as an intimate, high-accountability spiritual formation experience that helps participants follow Jesus more deeply while being discipled by a seasoned, globally respected leader. Through weekly engagement, personal mentoring, spiritual disciplines, life-on-life formation, and missional exposure, Fellows grow in every dimension of their lives.

Throughout the year, Fellows encounter a rich curriculum integrating tools such as Discovery Bible Study (DBS), Bible in One Year (BIOY), Emotionally Healthy Spirituality (EHS), Million Leaders Mandate (MLM), Africa to the Rest (A2R), What Would the Trinity Do? (WTTD), and other transformational frameworks.

The inaugural cohort of TSF commenced on January 3, 2025, meeting every Friday from 6:00 to 8:00 PM UTC without interruption for the full year, graduating in December 2025 at a ceremony that also inducted the 2026 cohort.

"Become a disciple of Jesus Christ who makes disciples."

The Curriculum Framework

The Kwiverr
Formation Konstant

The Shadrach Fellowship is built on the Kwiverr Formation Konstant, a comprehensive framework developed by Dr. Yaw Perbi that integrates four distinct but interconnected dimensions of holistic leader development.

The framework is Biblically-informed, Research-based, and Spirit-empowered, ensuring every Fellow grows spiritually, emotionally, relationally, and vocationally.

The 12-Month Journey

A Year of Transformation

Every week, every quarter, a carefully designed formation journey from induction to graduation.

Q1, January to March
Spiritual Formation
Rooting fellows in their identity in God before what they do for Him.
January: BIOY, 21-day fasting, DBS, Purpose Statements
February: WTTD, DISC self-awareness, Goal Setting
March: Spiritual Gift Assessment, DISC sharing
Q2, April to June
Missional Formation
Catching a vision for God's world and learning to make disciples.
April: Purpose discovery, Spiritual Gifts deep dive
May to June: Disciple Ship series (7 sessions)
June: Gospel sharing practicals, IBS Intro, EHS 1 and 2
Q3, July to September
Leadership Formation
The P3 Mid-Year Retreat, an immersive in-person intensive at Mamfe.
July: 3-Day P3 Retreat (EHS 3 to 5, DeepLead 1 to 5)
July to August: EHS 6 to 8, DeepLead series
August to September: Million Leaders Mandate series
Q4, October to December
Communal Formation
Marriage, money, missions, sent as community into their spheres.
October: Marriage and Money intensive, A2R begins
November to December: Africa to the Rest series
December 25: Graduation and new cohort induction

The Community Annex
TSFx 2026

The Shadrach Fellowship Annex (TSFx)

The Community Annex (TSFx) was created in 2026 because the quality of applications exceeded the core Fellowship's 12-person capacity. Rather than turn away extraordinary candidates, The HuD Group created a parallel track.

TSFx mentees receive the same curriculum as the core Fellowship, but are mentored directly by TSF 2025 graduates who are themselves mentees of Dr. Yaw Perbi. This ensures an unbroken chain of formation: Dr. Perbi trains TSF 2025 graduates, who in turn mentor TSFx 2026 mentees.

The Mentor-Mentee Chain
Dr. Yaw Perbi trains TSF 2025 graduates, who in turn mentor TSFx 2026 mentees using the same curriculum. A living, multiplying discipleship chain.

Theme: Peek to Peak

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to apply?
+
Individuals aged 17 to 39 who demonstrate faithfulness, availability, initiative, influence, teachability, hunger for God, and a readiness to be sent and obey Christ's Great Commission. Both men and women are welcome to apply.
Is the fellowship only for men?
+
No. The Shadrach Fellowship is fully open to both men and women who meet the eligibility criteria. The inaugural 2025 cohort included both men and women from across Africa.
How long does the fellowship last?
+
The fellowship lasts for one year, running from January to December. Fellows meet every Friday from 6:00 to 8:00 PM UTC without interruption, culminating in a graduation ceremony in December.
Will the fellowship be held online or in person?
+
The fellowship is held in a hybrid format. Weekly sessions are online via Zoom, with one in-person intensive retreat (the P3 Mid-Year Retreat) held in Ghana, typically in July.
What does the fellowship cost?
+
The fellowship is completely free of charge. Participants are expected to commit their time and full effort to engage in all sessions and assignments. For the in-person retreat, travel and accommodation support may be available for international fellows based on need.
What will I gain from the fellowship?
+
Participants gain personalised mentorship from Dr. Yaw Perbi, deeper spiritual growth, emotional maturity through EHS, practical life skills including on marriage and finances, leadership development, a heart for global missions, and lifelong community with a network of kingdom-minded leaders across Africa and beyond.
Can I participate while working or studying full time?
+
Yes. The fellowship is designed to be flexible and accommodate participants' schedules. Weekly sessions are on Friday evenings (6 to 8 PM UTC), making it accessible for working professionals and students. Full commitment and consistent attendance are expected.
What does the application process involve?
+
The process involves four steps: (1) Eligibility check against the criteria listed above, (2) Submitting an online application form, (3) Initial screening and shortlisting, and (4) Final interviews with Dr. Yaw Perbi and Frank Koomson before final selections are made.
How can I prepare for the fellowship?
+
Engage in daily prayer and Bible reading, reflect on your personal vision and mission, and maintain an openness to growth in all areas of life. Familiarise yourself with discipleship concepts and begin reading books on leadership and spiritual formation.
